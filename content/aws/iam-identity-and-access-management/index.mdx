

# IAM (Identity and Access Management) Overview

IAM (Identity and Access Management) is a service provided by Amazon AWS that allows you to securely control access to the resources in your AWS account. It helps you manage users, groups, and permissions to ensure that only authorized individuals or entities have access to your AWS services and data.

## User Management

IAM allows you to create and manage users within your AWS account. Users can be assigned unique credentials and permissions, allowing them to access specific AWS services and perform certain actions within those services.

## Group Management

Groups can be created within IAM to organize and manage multiple users at once. This makes it easier to assign permissions and access levels to a group of users rather than individually managing each one.

## Permissions and Policies

IAM uses policies to control access to AWS resources. These policies can be attached to users, groups, or roles, and specify what actions they are allowed to perform on specific resources. This allows you to grant different levels of access to different users and groups.

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds an extra layer of security to your AWS account by requiring users to provide a secondary form of authentication, such as a code from a mobile device, in addition to their username and password. This helps ensure that only authorized users can access your AWS services.

## Conclusion

IAM is an essential service for managing and securing access to your AWS resources. It allows you to create and manage users and groups, assign permissions, and add an extra layer of security with MFA. By using IAM, you can have better control over access to your AWS account and protect your data from unauthorized access.
